Machine learning (ML) represents a branch of artificial intelligence poised to revolutionize the modern era. Swift advancements in its underlying structures and algorithms, alongside the expansion of datasets, have resulted in enhanced computational capabilities across various domains. These encompass diverse areas like autonomous driving, language translation, chatbots, and even surpassing human proficiency in intricate board games such as Go. In this overview, we delve into the foundational concepts and algorithms that underpin machine learning, shedding light on specific methodologies for learning and optimization. Additionally, we provide insights into the applications of ML within the medical field. Specifically, we examine recent strides in diagnostic accuracy and potential limitations across domains such as dermatology, radiology, pathology, and general microscopy.
